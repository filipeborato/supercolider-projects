# SuperCollider Projects

This repository is a collection of **compositional studies** developed in **SuperCollider**, treated as a musical instrument rather than a technical framework.

In the same way that composers such as **Mozart, Chopin, or Bartók** produced études, variations, and sketches to explore specific musical problems at the piano or with small ensembles, this repository gathers studies that investigate **sound, gesture, form, and perception** through code.

The focus is not on finished works, but on **process, exploration, and musical thinking**.

---

## Concept

Each project functions as a study dedicated to one or more compositional questions, such as:

- How a single sound source can generate form over time
- How timbre can replace pitch as a primary structural element
- How spatial movement can act as musical gesture
- How controlled variation and constraint shape musical identity
- How repetition and deviation create perception of form

SuperCollider is used here as a **performative and compositional medium**, comparable to a traditional instrument used for technical and expressive studies.

---

## Musical Approach

- Emphasis on **process-based composition**
- Preference for **continuous transformation** over discrete events
- Exploration of **timbre, space, and density** as structural parameters
- Use of constraint and limited material to generate variation
- Avoidance of linear melodic or rhythmic development when not musically relevant

---

## Content

The repository may include:

- Short studies and sketches
- Parameter-driven variations of a single idea
- Synthesizer definitions used as compositional material
- Control routines shaping form and gesture
- Explorations of spatial and electroacoustic perception

Rendered audio files are intentionally excluded; the focus is on the **compositional logic**, not on fixed recordings.

---

## Requirements

- **SuperCollider** (3.13 or later)
- Headphones or a suitable listening setup for spatial perception
- No external libraries required unless explicitly noted

---
